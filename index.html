What 2019's Movies Tell Us: Drama, Blockbusters and Hype

A look at 500 films released in the pandemic era- Which genres dominated, which titles drew the most attention, and whether popularity really tracks with a good rating?

By Navya Asopa

2019 was a traumatic year for most as the global pandemic, COVID-19, had hit the whole wide world. But Netflix was having the time of its life. The OTT platform saw a surge in its number of subscribers, and started adding a whole lot of films and TV shows, spanning across genres, audiences, and popularity to its website. But what lies behind the subscription numbers in this pivotal year? What patterns, if any, defined our watchlist and while we were locked inside our homes, which genres dominated our screens, which titles captured our undivided attention, and did popularity actually reflect the content quality? Here’s an analysis of 500 Netflix titles in 2019, with their genre distribution, vote count, vote average, and popularity scores.

Drama topped 2019 genre charts, followed by comedy and thriller, which indicates that emotionally-driven narratives and fast-paced storytelling made up the bulk of Netflix’s strategy. The platform’s catalog also featured animation, fantasy and romance which appeared less frequently on the list.

Some films and shows that became a cult-favorite ranged from superhero films to animations. “Avengers: Endgame” and “Frozen II” stood high up the list, and the occasional horror picks like “It Chapter Two” also made it to top 10. This shows that the most-watched titles of 2019 were dominated by blockbuster films and superhero sequels, which Netflix relied on to draw in large audiences.

This plot tells a slight different story- One would assume that the more popular a film is, the more its average viewer rating would be. But when popularity score was paired with the average viewer ratings for 2019 titles, the relationship looked uneven. While some highly popular films and shows also received strong ratings by the audience, many did not. Perhaps, popularity is not directly correlated with content quality or audience approval. Something to think about when you watch the next most talked-about film on Netflix!
